Background

Nepal's air quality in major cities is not always healthy. I did not always look at this from the data perspective, but being aware of it has really driven my desire to do so. For the purposes of this exploration, I will not focus on the sources, but just look at what data is available, and what is the quality of publicly available data.

The dataset here is from the Government of Nepal itself containing daily values from different stations. I greatly appreciate the open data sharing policy adopted by the Department of Environment which allows all concerned to take the data and understand it for ourselves. Let's begin.

Summary

From November to April in almost 100 % of the days, pollution levels at Khumaltar station did not meet the WHO Interim Target I.

From May to October, the situation improves quite well - potentially helped by monsoon.

This analysis was for Khumaltar, but it could be repeated for any of the stations in the major cities.

The stations responsible for data collection should do better to get this data for all the days of the year.

Finally, plots here can be compared with Figures 70, 72, 73 and 74 of the 2022 report published by the Department of Environment, Nepal.
